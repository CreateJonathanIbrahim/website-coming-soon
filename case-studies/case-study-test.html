Overview

Building Operational Infrastructure for a Scaling Agency

Client / Context

WISE Digital Partners, Austin TX — a full-service digital marketing agency scaling rapidly across design, development, SEO, content, and paid media.

The Challenge

Six departments operating in isolated silos across Jira, Asana, Monday.com, and an endless web of Google Sheets. Cross-departmental handoffs were failing silently, leadership had zero real-time visibility into capacity, and account managers were spending 15 hours per week manually compiling status reports.

My Role

Lead Solutions Architect and Change Manager. Responsible for auditing the company's full operational footprint, designing a unified workspace taxonomy, configuring the entire ClickUp environment, and leading change management for 52 remote employees across 4 continents.

The Outcome

Deprecated three legacy SaaS tools, eliminating $14K in annual spend. Achieved 100% ClickUp adoption in 60 days. Reduced project delivery delays by 34% within the first quarter post-launch.

The Challenge

The Cost of Operational Debt

The core issue wasn't just tool fatigue — it was a fundamental collapse of systemic trust. When data lives in five different places, truth becomes subjective. Every department had its own definition of "done," and none of them matched.

For example: the Design team marked a task complete in Asana, but the Development team in Jira had no idea it was ready to be picked up — because the Zapier integration between the two platforms had quietly broken three months earlier and nobody noticed. Account Managers were spending 15 hours a week manually consolidating spreadsheets just to run Monday morning status meetings. Leadership was flying blind on capacity, unable to answer "what's at risk this week?" without a 30-minute manual audit.

The Approach

How I Solved It

01

Discovery & Audit

Conducted structured 1:1 interviews with all 6 department heads and 12 individual contributors. Mapped the full lifecycle of a client deliverable — from sales closed-won to final deployment — identifying 14 critical bottleneck points and 7 recurring failure modes in cross-departmental handoffs.

Stakeholder interview mapping and workflow audit documentation

02

Architecture Design

Designed a strictly enforced Space › Folder › List taxonomy that mirrored the company's real org structure, not an idealized one. Standardized 22 custom fields at the Workspace level to guarantee data continuity across departments — the foundation that makes executive dashboards pull cross-departmental metrics without breaking.

Workspace taxonomy diagram and custom field schema

03

Build & Configuration

Built the full ClickUp environment from the ground up — 6 department Spaces, standardized List templates for every project type, and 40+ automation rules handling cross-departmental handoffs, status updates, and due-date escalations. Integrated with Slack via API for real-time notifications and connected Make.com webhooks for advanced calculations beyond ClickUp's native formula limits.

Automation flow configuration and Slack integration setup

04

Training & Launch

Technology doesn't solve culture — people do. Rolled out the system in three phased cohorts, starting with department heads and ClickUp Champions before opening to the full team. Created 18 role-specific Loom training videos, hosted daily office hours for the first 30 days, and built a searchable internal SOP library so no one had to ask the same question twice.

For the Curious

Technical Deep Dive

To ensure reporting didn't break at the Space level, all 22 custom fields were created at the Workspace level and selectively pinned to relevant Spaces — not duplicated. This is a critical but often-overlooked distinction: duplicate fields fragment reporting.

The taxonomy enforced a strict three-tier hierarchy: Space (department) → Folder (client or initiative) → List (project phase or deliverable type). This mirrors how people think about their work, which is why adoption was fast.

Key fields standardized globally: Project Phase, Department Owner, Client Name, Due Date Tier, Escalation Flag, and Revenue Impact. These six fields power 80% of the executive dashboard logic.

ClickUp's native automations handled the straightforward triggers (status changes → Slack pings, due date approaching → assignee notification). But for anything requiring cross-list logic or multi-step calculations, I routed through Make.com via ClickUp's webhook API.

The most complex scenario: when a task's "Phase" dropdown changed to "Ready for Dev," a Make.com scenario fired — pulling the assigned developer's current open task count, calculating remaining sprint capacity against a target threshold, and writing the result back into a hidden ClickUp metadata field. That field fed the executive capacity dashboard without requiring manual input from anyone.

Total: 14 Make.com scenarios handling edge cases that ClickUp's native rules couldn't reach. Each scenario documented in the SOP library with a visual flow diagram.

The system was rolled out in three cohorts over six weeks — not all at once. Cohort 1: Department Heads (week 1-2). Cohort 2: ClickUp Champions — one per department, selected for influence, not seniority (week 3-4). Cohort 3: Full team (week 5-6).

For each department, I created role-specific Loom walkthroughs: what their Space looks like, how to log work, how handoffs work, and where to find help. 18 videos total, averaging 4 minutes each. Zero generic tutorials — every video was specific to a role's actual workflow.

Daily office hours ran for the first 30 days. The Champions handled day-to-day questions within their departments; I handled escalations and edge cases. By day 45, office hours dropped to twice weekly. By day 60, we formally closed the support period with 100% active usage across all 52 users.
