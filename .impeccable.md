# .impeccable.md — Jonathan Ibrahim Personal Site

## Design Context

### Users

**Now:** Hiring managers and recruiters who've landed here via LinkedIn, a cold email, or a referral. They're evaluating Jonathan quickly — scanning for signal, not reading every word. They're on a desktop at work, or a phone during a commute. They've seen dozens of portfolio sites this week and most of them look the same.

**Eventually:** A broader audience of practitioners, ops leaders, and aspiring operators who come for the insights, frameworks, and expertise content. Think newsletter readers, LinkedIn followers, people who find the site via a shared article.

**Job to be done:** Quickly form a high-confidence impression of Jonathan as someone strategic, unusually capable, and worth their time.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Strategic. Personable. Intelligent.

**Voice:** Confident without being arrogant. Warm but not casual. The writing sounds like a sharp person talking, not a résumé or a press release. Jonathan has strong opinions formed from real experience — the site should feel like that.

**Emotional goal:** Surprise + delight. "Wow, I wasn't expecting this" — not from flashiness, but from polish, depth, and the sense that someone genuinely thoughtful built this. The site should feel categorically different from every other ops/PM portfolio.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark only. Near-black backgrounds, teal accent (#64a19d). This is non-negotiable and already established.

**Visual tone:** Refined editorial. Not corporate, not startup-bro, not minimalist-for-minimalism's-sake. Think: a well-designed business magazine from a decade ago that has aged gracefully — confident typographic hierarchy, intelligent use of whitespace, purposeful restraint with occasional moments of visual richness.

**Anti-references (from project docs):**
- No glassmorphism, no glow borders
- No gradient text
- No side-stripe card accents
- No identical card grids with icon + heading + text
- No hero metric layout (big number, small label, gradient accent)
- No purple (#7464a1 is banned)
- No AI-palette aesthetics: cyan-on-dark, neon on dark, purple-to-blue gradients

**Colors:**
- Accent (Teal): `#64a19d` — use sparingly, not everywhere
- Backgrounds: `#050505`, `#0d0d0d`, `#000` (tint toward teal, never pure black)
- Text: `#fff`, `#ddd`, `#aaa`
- Borders: `#1a1a1a`, `#222`, `#333`

**Existing fonts (preserve unless explicitly redesigning):**
- Varela Round — headings and labels
- Nunito — body text

**WCAG AA** accessibility baseline required.

### Design Principles

1. **Earn the surprise.** The "wow" moment comes from unexpected polish and depth, not decoration. Every design decision should make the content feel more valuable, not more decorated.

2. **Teal is the reward, not the wallpaper.** Accent color appears at moments of genuine emphasis — CTAs, key stats, active states. When it's everywhere, it's nowhere.

3. **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Hierarchy, weight, and scale should communicate authority before the visitor reads a word. A recruiter should feel they're on a senior person's site within 3 seconds.

4. **Asymmetry over templates.** Sections should feel composed, not generated. Vary rhythm: tight and dense in one section, open and breathable in the next.

5. **Mobile is a first-class context.** A recruiter reviewing candidates on their phone is a real scenario. Nothing collapses into unreadability; nothing gets hidden that matters.
