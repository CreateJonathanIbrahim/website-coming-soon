Origin Story

The background that doesn't fit neatly on a résumé

My dad immigrated from Lebanon to Romania for medical school. He met my mom there — also a doctor. They eventually made their way to Chicago, where I was born. First-generation American, raised between cultures, in a household where precision and excellence weren't suggestions.

Growing up, I was always around technology — not just using it, but fascinated by it. At 13, I built my first website on GeoCities. Not because anyone asked me to. Just because I wanted to see if I could.

"I built my first website at 13. Not because anyone asked me to. Just because I wanted to see if I could."

By 22, I was freelancing — building websites for clients across industries, learning the craft hands-on. In 2019, I founded Electrician Metrics, a digital marketing business serving electrical contractors. I thought my edge was web design and SEO.

What I actually learned was something more valuable: my real superpower was translating between worlds. Between what a client needs and what a developer understands. Between a messy backlog and a shipped product. Between strategic intent and operational execution. That realization changed everything.

What Drives Me

I can't help it. I just want everything to be better.

It's not a professional trait. It's closer to a compulsion. I walk into a room and immediately start noticing what's inefficient, what's missing, what could be tighter. I look at a process and ask: what's the bottleneck? I look at a team and ask: what's slowing them down?

My attention to detail isn't something I turn on for work. It's just how I operate. Every ounce of productivity and effectiveness I can wring out of a system — I want it. Not for the metrics. Because leaving potential on the table genuinely bothers me.

"My attention to detail isn't something I turn on for work. It's just how I operate."

What makes this useful professionally is the bridge it creates. I speak client. I speak developer. I speak designer, ops lead, and C-suite. Most people are fluent in one or two of those. That translation — from ambiguous client need to a specific, shippable task — is where I live.

Beyond the Work

The version of me that doesn't show up on LinkedIn

How I Work

A few things I won't negotiate on

01

Honesty, always.

I'm not diplomatic for the sake of making people comfortable. I'll tell you what I actually think — clearly and directly. Not unkindly, but plainly. The truth is almost always the fastest path forward.

02

Clarity over cleverness.

A good system isn't impressive — it's invisible. The best operations infrastructure is the kind that everyone can understand, follow, and improve without needing me in the room.

03

Finish what you start.

I have deep respect for completion. Strategy without execution is just creative writing. I care about shipping, and about the craft it takes to get there without cutting corners.

04

Leave it better than you found it.

Every project I take on, every team I work with, every system I touch — I want it to be meaningfully better by the time I'm done. That's not ambition. That's just the standard.
